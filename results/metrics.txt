Quantum spin reservoir time-series comparison

Same QRC settings for both tasks:
- 5 qubits
- 7 virtual measurement nodes per input
- evolution time 0.65
- ridge 0.0001
- Hamiltonian seed 3
- density-matrix state rho with shape (32, 32)

Task results:

Mackey-Glass:
- QRC one-step RMSE, normalized scale:       0.005254
- QRC one-step RMSE, original scale:         0.001240
- Memoryless one-step baseline RMSE:         0.146814
- Short free-run QRC RMSE, normalized scale: 0.077236
- Short free-run QRC RMSE, original scale:   0.018229

NARMA10:
- QRC one-step RMSE, normalized scale:       0.649665
- QRC one-step RMSE, original scale:         0.070224
- Memoryless one-step baseline RMSE:         0.836093

Takeaway:
- Mackey-Glass is predicted very accurately by this small QRC.
- NARMA10 is harder for the same reservoir settings.
- This is the lesson: reservoir performance depends on the task,
  even when the reservoir architecture is unchanged.

Notes:
- The reservoir is a quantum spin system.
